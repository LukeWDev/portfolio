Technical Showcase

Dynamic Tick & AkComponent Spawning

Optimisation

Horizon Call of the Mountain

In order to minimise the audio footprint, a few key systems were implemented to reduce the number of updates that occur every frame, and minimise the number of AkComponents that are present in the game world.

A dynamic tick system that was implemented into the base audio class allowed us to adjust the number of intervals and the spacing between each interval, in order to retain accurate updates for responsive gameplay, whilst reducing necessary updates.

To reduce the number of AkComponents that are present in the game world, a dynamic spawning system was used to destroy AkComponents that were outside of the attenuation radius; with the option of adding a delay before destruction to reduce spamming if the player walks in and out of the radius.

Combat Stem Ducking

Music System

Horizon Call of the Mountain

To preserve the sensation of large, formidable machines in a first person perspective, it was crucial to convey the weight of machine attacks during combat. To help achieve this, a stem ducking system was implemented into the music system which allowed per stem EQ and volume control during machine attacks.

By employing this system, we were able to attenuate clashing low-end frequencies in stems such as low percussion or low synths. This allowed us to maintain the integrity of the overall score while simultaneously upholding the intensity of the combat experience.

Ambient Music

Music System

Horizon Call of the Mountain

While the gameplay path follows a linear trajectory, it offers immersive areas to explore, teeming with rich lore, physics-based collectables, interactive instruments, and breathtaking vistas. It became evident that these design elements, combined with the physically demanding nature of VR climbing and the need for designated rest points, necessitated additional ambient music tracks that adapt to the player's location and duration of stay in each specific area.

To address this, an advanced system of ambient zones was implemented throughout the game world. These zones can be strategically positioned and configured to trigger different music tracks based on varying time intervals, incorporating the option of random offsets. These tracks are played at a lower priority compared to story music, serving as supplementary accompaniment for non-story interactions.

This intricate music system enhances the player's engagement by seamlessly blending with the environment, creating a cohesive audio backdrop that aligns with the unfolding gameplay experience.

Independent Hand Routing

Haptics System

Horizon Call of the Mountain

Among the notable technical hurdles, one of the most significant involved integrating haptics into the cutting-edge VR2 controllers. To address this challenge, a system was implemented to facilitate hand haptic routing. This ensured that player interactions were distinctly felt, with the haptic feedback being responsive to the specific connecting hand(s). Additionally, in-world occurrences such as explosions or machine attacks were dynamically conveyed based on the proximity of each hand.

This system is used across multiple pillars of the game, including physics interactions, reverberations from gong impacts, the sensation of being in close proximity to a campfire, thrilling combat events, and countless other instances.

Dynamic Wind System

Ambience System

Horizon Call of the Mountain

In order to immerse players fully within the captivating and lively world of Horizon, harnessing the capabilities of PS5's 3D audio was paramount. A pivotal aspect of achieving this goal involved implementing a dynamic wind system that envelops the player, intensifying as they ascend towering peaks.

This system employs the generation of wind emitters that dynamically spawn and traverse a predefined spline, adhering to carefully calibrated parameters such as speed, direction, and distant offset settings. This creates a rich, multi-layered, 3D experience that seamlessly adapts to the player's movements.
